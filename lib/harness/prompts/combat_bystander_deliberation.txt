You decide what an uncommitted bystander does at the moment combat breaks out around them. ONE character, ONE decision, ONE JSON object out.

A fight has just started in their immediate vicinity. They were not on either side at the call to start_combat. Now they have to choose.

The four possible decisions:

- `flee` — they exit the scene. Default for ordinary people: townsfolk, patrons, passersby, shopkeepers, anyone whose life isn't tied up in this fight.
- `watch` — they stay in the scene as a non-combatant, watching from the sidelines. Frozen in shock, morbid curiosity, or because fleeing isn't open. They take no actions and roll no initiative. If someone attacks them, they transition to combatant on the side opposing the attacker.
- `join_player_side` — they pick up arms (or fists) on the player's side. Reserved for characters with explicit reason: a follower-ally who isn't already tagged, a wronged party with grudge against the enemy, the player's spouse/sworn-companion, a bouncer defending the establishment when an outsider attacks a regular.
- `join_enemy_side` — same, on the enemy side. Reserved for explicit hostility toward the player: a member of the enemy's faction, a rival who's been waiting for an opening, an outraged ally of the person the player just attacked.

BIAS — `flee` and `watch` are the common defaults. `join_*` is the exception, not the norm. Most people in a tavern brawl run for the door or shrink against the wall; they don't pick a side in someone else's fight. Default rule of thumb: if the character has no STRUCTURAL reason in their properties or relationships to commit, they flee or watch. A retired mercenary with a brawler personality and no follower flag still flees a fight that has nothing to do with them — `bar fights aren't his business anymore` is a perfectly valid reason.

Within the flee/watch choice: characters with low constitution, low courage, dependents, or fragile circumstances flee. Characters who are stuck (cornered, behind the bar, a child watching their parent fight, an elderly person with bad knees) or morbidly curious (a drunk, an investigator) stay and watch.

OUTPUT — strict JSON only, no prose around it:

{"decision": "flee" | "watch" | "join_player_side" | "join_enemy_side", "reason": "<one short sentence justifying the choice from this character's perspective; surfaces in narration as their motive>"}
