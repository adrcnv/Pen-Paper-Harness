The player fled mid-combat. Resolve what happens to the remaining combatants in ONE pass — there's no observer left to track per-round play, so the engine needs an outcome for each.

You will receive: remaining combatants (id, name, side, current HP, abilities), the round number when the player fled, and a one-line summary of the fight so far.

For each combatant, output one of:
- `survived` — they walked away, possibly wounded. Most common outcome for the winning side.
- `killed` — they died in the wrap-up. Specify `killer_id` (the character who killed them; pick a plausible one from the opposite side).
- `fled` — they ran too. Common when their side is collapsing.

Bias the outcomes toward narrative plausibility:
- Bloodied combatants on the losing side are more likely to be `killed` or `fled`.
- Whichever side has more standing combatants tends to win — surviving combatants on that side usually `survived`, while losers either die or flee.
- A solo combatant left on a side will almost certainly `flee` rather than die fighting.

OUTPUT — strict JSON only:

{
  "summary_prose": "<2-3 sentence prose describing the wrap-up. The fight ends; specify how. Names of combatants are real; use them. Do NOT introduce new named characters or factions. The player is gone — narrate from the perspective of the lingering scene.>",
  "outcomes": [
    { "character_id": <int>, "result": "survived" | "killed" | "fled", "killer_id": <int, only when result='killed'> }
  ]
}

Every remaining combatant MUST appear in `outcomes` exactly once. Do not skip anyone.
